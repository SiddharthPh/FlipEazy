How to leverage content to scale up your newly purchased website?

Content Creation.

We’ve all come across people who tell us to create content if you want to scale up.

And guess what?

They are absolutely right. Upping your content game is something you must always strive for.

When you own a content-based website, it is important that you cultivate the habit of creating content once a week or so.

Sometimes, for most people, they are satisfied with the site that is generating passive income and they are not interested in experimenting with new stuff.

But, the truth is they are going to lose more than gain. How?

Google hates websites that are not keeping themselves updated every now and then and it penalizes them by pushing their websites down the search results.

Bad, right?

Is content-creation really that powerful?

Yes, indeed.

When you buy a website, your main fear is you do not want your rankings and traffic to diminish.

What do you do to keep up this stability?

Well, some people might adhere to creating backlinks, use CRO software to increase conversions, or concentrate on off-page SEO.

But you must choose to take that path only if you consider yourself a pro and have hands-on experience in those domains.

Otherwise, it might cost you a lot.

But if you choose to take the Content-creation path there is always a positive ROI.

Why?

Because when you are an authoritative site for a particular niche, any new content you post will be rewarded by Google in the form of high rankings.

The authority juice that was already existing will be passed on to the new content post and eventually rank much higher without any link building activity.

Quick Fixes :

When you purchase a new website there are always certain things you can take advantage of related to content.

Sometimes the seller, though aware of such things, will turn a blind eye, thinking that it is not much of importance.

But guess what?

You can always use these “Quick fixes” to your advantage.

Want to know how?

Let us dive in,

Fix#1 Optimizing Old content :

One of the best ways to improve your rankings is to optimize keywords that are under-utilized.

The strategy, if implemented in the right way can show you results in no time.

If an article is ranking for a particular keyword, Google usually shows your article in the search results for that keyword, but it might also show the same result when a keyword that is similar to your existing keyword is being searched, though the keywords have not been mentioned in the article.

But the site must be ranking on page 2 for a similar keyword, only because that keyword has not been mentioned in the article.

These keywords are what we call Under-utilized keywords.

So what you can do is, try adding those relevant keywords into your article and see the magic happen. Google will push your website on top of the search results, resulting in a boost in traffic.

Fix#2 Keyword Cannibalization :

Sometimes, most of the websites can be a victim of keyword cannibalization, If ignored it can be a nightmare for most of the business owners.

So,

What exactly is Keyword Cannibalization?

It is a situation where multiple pages on your website end up ranking for a similar keyword.

And why is that a big deal? Will that not increase my traffic?

Well,

Google, as a webpage crawler wants to understand each and every information present on your page and rank it accordingly, but when it stumbles upon two pages that contain similar content it gets confused and ends up ranking none of your articles.

How to overcome the situation of keyword cannibalization?

play_arrowRe-Optimizing Pages :

Though you have caused the cannibalization to happen accidentally, you can always fix this issue by Re-optimizing or De-optimizing the pages.

This usually happens for e-commerce websites. Let us say you are selling a product which has 4 different colors, and it is quite common to notice that, though they have unique urls, it is quite evident that they have a similar title tag, H1 tag and also there is no clear-cut differentiation among all the four.

So you can actually re-optimize the pages to show some kind of differentiation and can avoid cannibalization issues.

play_arrowCanonical Tags :

Canonical tags are the best way to avoid any kind of cannibalization issues.

What does it do?

When you have two pages ranking for a similar term, it is referred to as content duplication. When you create a canonical tag from a least important page to the other, you basically are telling google to rank the most important page that is driving all the traffic.

It helps in ranking the page you want to rank.

There are many more ways to avoid cannibalization, if you want more info please refer to this article by Semrush.

Pumping in additional content to your new website.

play_arrowCompetitive analysis:

When you are planning to pump in some new articles to your website it is important to understand whether there are any content gaps.

So tools like ahrefs and semrush kind of make this job easier by helping you understand what are the potential keywords that your competitors are ranking for and you can capitalize on these keywords to your advantage.

The purpose of doing this is to understand what topics are to be covered, and how you can pivot towards various sub-niches.

play_arrowInternal Linking:

This is basically done to improve your website navigation for your visitors. It actually determines how well its site is structured.

If there are articles that drive the most of the traffic and do not have proper internal linking, you can actually fix by adding posts relevant to those articles and add internal links in the money making posts which in turn helps driving that traffic to your other informational posts.

This practice helps your articles that are ranking in the 2nd page to land on the first page of the search result.

While building internal links don’t randomly place them, instead understand the topic of the article and then if you have an article that supports the other article then it must be utilized for building internal links so that the link juice is passed on.

play_arrowHorizontal Content Creation:

When you are in a niche, once you are done with writing all the articles on mainstream topics, it is also important to write articles on sub-niches that can support the mainstream topics.

For example: Let us your primary article focus on “Best laptops of 2020” now what you can do is pivot to a sub-niche and you can probably write an article that focuses on “Best laptops for gaming in 2020”.

Now, you got yourself a sub-niche that is Laptops for gaming and you can keep building on the sub-niche and now you have created yourself an opportunity for horizontal growth.

CONCLUSION :

We hope we’ve given you all the necessary information on how you can leverage content to grow your newly purchased website.

To summarize the whole post:

play_arrow We’ve mentioned how you can capitalize on the quick fixes that is, how to optimize or update old content and we’ve also mentioned how you can fix keyword cannibalization.

play_arrow We’ve covered the added advantage of creating content for your sub-niches and how you can rank them in the first page without the need of backlinks

play_arrow We also shared the importance of website structuring through internal linking that can help your website rank on page1.

Leveraging content to your newly purchased website is the most important aspect to focus on if you want to scale and turn it into a 6-figure business model.
